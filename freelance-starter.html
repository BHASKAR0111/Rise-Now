Your First ₹1,000 Online

A strictly actionable, 7-day blueprint to land your first remote gig or freelance client. Zero theory. Pure execution.

The 7-Day Sprint
0 / 7 Completed
✓
Day 1: Build a Micro-Portfolio (3 Projects)
Clients don't care about your degree, they care what you can build. Spend exactly 8 hours building 3 incredibly specific, high-quality projects. Host them on Vercel, Netlify, or Dribbble.
Create free Vercel account →
✓
Day 2: Set up specialized profiles on Fiverr & Upwork
Do not be a "Web Developer." Be a "Shopify Landing Page Expert for D2C Brands." Niche down so hard it physically hurts. Write your bio entirely about the *client's* problems.
Join Upwork →
✓
Day 3: The Cold Outreach Protocol
Go to Instagram/LinkedIn and find 10 small business owners offering bad quality services (bad websites, ugly graphics, slow load times). Send them this exact script:
"Hey [Name], I noticed your site takes 4s to load on mobile. I actually rebuilt your homepage on my local server and got it down to 0.8s. I made a 2min Loom video showing you how to fix it for free. Link here."
✓
Day 4: Join high-signal remote communities
The best jobs aren't on job boards, they are in private Slack/Discord communities.
Check Wellfound (formerly AngelList) →
✓
Day 5: Offer a "free sample" to your dream client
Find a startup you love. Do 2 hours of work for them entirely for free (e.g., redesign a screen, clean up a dataset). Email the founder directly. Tell them if they like it, they can pay you ₹1,000 to do the rest.
✓
Day 6: Pitch 20 gigs on Upwork using the "Loom Hack"
Don't write proposals. Record a 60-second Loom video of yourself speaking directly to the client's job post. Link it in the first line of your proposal. You will stand out from 99% of bots.
✓
Day 7: Follow up ruthlessly
The fortune is in the follow-up. Reply to everyone you contacted on Day 3 and Day 5. Ask for 10 minutes of their time. Close the deal.
